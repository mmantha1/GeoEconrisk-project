Taiwan's Hsinchu Science Park is a critical nexus point for international trade, producing over 60% of the world's advanced semiconductor chips. However, environmental assessments indicate high climate physical exposure. Specifically, coastal infrastructure faces severe flooding vulnerabilities from category 4 and 5 typhoons, which are projected to increase by 22% under SSP5-8.5 warming models by 2040. Grid vulnerability is also high, as extreme heatwaves tax localized power lines and substations, presenting localized operational risks.
